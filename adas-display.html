Branded ADAS System Names

What is ADAS?

ADAS Camera Systems

ADAS camera systems are used to help the driver and vehicle respond to external factors within the driving environment. Cameras are usually located against the windshield glass, around the rearview mirrors, and on the trunk of the vehicle. These cameras can detect other vehicles, obstacles, traffic signs and signals, lane lines, and pedestrians. This data is used to make intelligent safety warnings and take preventative actions, such as lane departure warnings, emergency braking, blind spot alerts, and parking assistance.

ADAS Radar Systems

ADAS radar systems are used to help the driver and vehicle recognize external factors within the driving environment. Unlike cameras, radar systems work continue to work efficiently even in poor weather or low-lighting conditions. Radar systems are usually located within the vehicle, whether that be inside the bumper, in the back corners, and the trunk of the vehicle. Radars work by emitting radio waves, and measuring how long it takes for them to bounce back. This allows the vehicle's computer to calculate the distance, speed, and direction of nearby objects. Radar technology can detect other vehicles, obstacles, animals, and pedestrians. This data is used to make intelligent safety warnings and take preventative actions, such as adaptive cruise control, blind spot alerts, and parking assistance.

ADAS Ultrasound Systems

A cheaper alternative to radar, ultrasonic sensor technology generates high-frequency audio waves, which allows for the sensor to listen for reflections that return from nearby objects. Due to their limited scope, ultrasonic sensors are used primarily for close-range features such as park assist.

Front-Facing Lidar

Time to Place Targets for Calibration

OEM Time: 25 mins

Hunter Ultimate ADAS® Time: 3:30 mins

What It Is

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) technology uses lasers to measure distances, and create detailed 3D maps of the surrounding environment based on the reflections. The Doppler effect provides a physics-based, reliable way to measure the velocity of an oncoming object. LiDAR can be used to detect pedestrians, cyclists, animals, and other vehicles, even in poor lighting conditions.

What It Does

LiDAR compliments cameras systems and radar to assist with automatic emergency braking, lane departure warning, adaptive cruise control, lane keeping, and pedestrian detection.
